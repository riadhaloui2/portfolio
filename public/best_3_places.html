Top 3 Places Visited By Riadh

My best 3 places :

1 - Ain Drahem

A town in northwestern Tunisia, located in the Jendouba Governorate.
Ain Draham is known for its beautiful natural surroundings,
including forests and mountains, making it a popular destination for tourists,
especially those interested in outdoor activities like hiking and trekking.

2 - Medinah

city of Medina, also spelled "Medinah" or "Madinah."
Medina is one of the holiest cities in Islam, located in the Hejaz region of Saudi Arabia.
It is the second holiest city after Mecca and holds great significance for Muslims worldwide. Medina is renowned as the city to which the Prophet Muhammad migrated from Mecca in 622 CE, marking the beginning of the Islamic calendar.
The city contains many important historical sites, including the Prophet's Mosque (Al-Masjid an-Nabawi), which is built around the tomb of the Prophet Muhammad and is one of the largest mosques in the world.
Every year, millions of Muslims undertake the pilgrimage (Hajj) to Mecca, and many also visit Medina as part of their religious journey. Medina's rich history and religious importance make it a focal point for Muslims seeking spiritual fulfillment and understanding of Islamic traditions.

3 - Mekkah

Mecca (also spelled Makkah), the birthplace of the Prophet Muhammad and the holiest city in Islam.
It is located in the western region of Saudi Arabia's Hejaz, about 70 kilometers inland from the Red Sea coast.
Mecca is the focal point of the Islamic pilgrimage (Hajj), which Muslims are required to undertake at least once in their lifetime if they are physically and financially able.
The pilgrimage includes rituals such as circumambulating the Kaaba, a cube-shaped structure located within the Masjid al-Haram (the Sacred Mosque), and performing various prayers and acts of worship at specific sites in and around the city.
Mecca holds immense spiritual significance for Muslims around the world, as it is believed to be the place where the Prophet Muhammad received the first revelations of the Quran from Allah through the Angel Gabriel.
The city attracts millions of pilgrims annually, making it one of the most visited religious sites globally. Aside from its religious importance, Mecca is also a center for Islamic scholarship and education, with many universities and institutions dedicated to the study of Islam and its teachings.
